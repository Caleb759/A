PESTS IN NZ

    The most common pests in New Zealand are:

  • MICE
  • OPOSSUMS
  • RABBITS
  • STOATS
  • WEASELS

MICE:

Mice can negatively impact the environment by preying on native species, competing for resources, damaging vegetation, and potentially spreading diseases. Their ability to reproduce rapidly can lead to population explosions, exacerbating these issues.

OPPOSUMS:

Opossums can be detrimental to the environment due to their potential to spread diseases, disrupt ecosystems, and damage property. They are known to carry diseases like bovine tuberculosis, which can affect livestock and other animals, and their consumption of native plants and animals can negatively impact biodiversity. Additionally, opossums can cause structural damage to buildings and gardens.

RABBITS:

Rabbits can be bad for the environment due to their destructive feeding habits, leading to soil erosion, habitat damage, and competition with native species. They are also known to carry diseases that can affect other native species.

STOATS:

Stoats are dangerous to the New Zealand environment because they are voracious predators of native bird populations, including those that nest on the ground or in tree cavities. They also impact other native species like lizards, frogs, and insects. Stoats were introduced to New Zealand to control rabbits but have since become a major threat to native wildlife.

WEASELS:

Weasels are considered harmful to the environment in many regions, particularly in New Zealand, because like weasels, they are effective predators of native birds, lizards, and insects, disrupting local ecosystems. They were introduced to control rabbits but have become a significant pest, contributing to the decline of native species.
